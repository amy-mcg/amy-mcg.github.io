Amy McGuire

Department of Geography, University of Cambridge am2522@cam.ac.uk

I am a physical geographer, interested in the nature of vegetation responses to abrupt climate oscillations. In particular, I am interested in the ways we can accurately correlate records of past climate change to better understand the spatial variability in ecosystem response.

Education

University of Cambridge

PhD Geography

Title: Resolving abrupt palaeoenvironmental changes in a lake sediment sequence from Ioannina, northwest Greece

My research reconstructs the vegetation response to abrupt climate change using lake sediments from Ioannina, northwest Greece for the period 45-40 ka BP. This involved production of a new chronology for the sequence using tephra (volcanic ash) layers and the application novel radiocarbon dating methods in addition to sub-centennial scale vegetation reconstruction through high-resolution palynology. In doing so, I sought to develop understanding of the temporal and spatial variation in vegetation responses.

Primary supervisor: Prof Christine Lane (University of Cambridge)
External supervisors: Dr Katy Roucoux and Dr Ian Lawson (University of St. Andrews)

Tephrochron

    Tephrochron is an R package designed to aid in the analysis and presentation of tephrochronological data. The package is currently under development, and can be accessed here.

    Install the latest version of this package by entering the following in R:

    install.packages("remotes")
remotes::install_github("amy-mcg/tephrochron")

    If you have any questions or ideas for useful features, please get in touch at am2522@cam.ac.uk!
