Pollen Extraction

Subsampling
1. Sample desired amount of sediment (ca. 1 cm3) using a volumetric sampler or using water displacement in a 5 cm3 measuring cylinder, placed into a 15 ml plastic centrifuge tube
2. Add an exotic Lycopodium marker spore tablet to quantify the concentration of pollen within the samples
Carbonate removal
3. Heat up water bath in fume hood to ca. 90°C
4. Add 30 ml 7% HCl to each sample and place in water bath for 20-30 mins, in clay rich samples stir occasionally with a glass rod (one per tube)
5. Whirlimix, balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant
6. To remove HCl remainder whirlimix and top up with DI water
7. Balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant into a waste bottle
Humic acid and fine sediment removal
8. Add 25 ml 10% NaOH to each sample and place in water bath for 2-4 mins
9. Sieve at 125 μm, washing through using DI water into a clean 1 L borosilicate beaker
10. Sieve the < 125 μm fraction, now in the beaker, at 6 μm
11. Rinse the 6-125 μm fraction from the mesh back into the centrifuge tube using distilled water
12. Balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant
Silicate removal
13. Prepare suitable volume of SPT (sodium polytungstate) solution to a specific gravity between 1.89 and 1.91 g cm−3, allowing for ca. 8 ml per sample
14. Add 4 ml of the prepared SPT solution to each sample
15. Whirlimix, balance and centrifuge at 1800 rpm for 20 mins with the break off (deceleration >3)
16. Pour supernatant into a new 50 ml nalgene centrifuge tube, topped up with DI water to within 5 ml of the top of the tube (reducing the overall density to around 1.15–1.2 g cm−3), cap, and mix by performing 8–10 inversions by hand
17. Repeat steps 14-16
18. Centrifuge tubes containing the supernatant at 3000 rpm for 3 mins. Decant supernatant into a 1 L borosilicate beaker for later recycling of SPT
19. Top up samples with deionized water and centrifuge and decant again
Acetolysis
20. Whirlimix samples and top up with glacial acetic acid (CH3COOH)
21. Balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant into a waste bottle
22. Make up the acetolysis mixture by combining 9 parts of acetic anhydride with 1 part of conc. sulphuric acid
23. Place 5 ml of the acetolysis, working in the fume cupboard, and incubate in waterbath for 3 mins
24. Top up with glacial acetic acid (CH3COOH) to stop the reaction
25. Balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant into a waste bottle
26. To remove any acetolysis remainder whirlimix and top up with DI water
27. Balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant
Slide mounting
28. OPTIONAL Wash with a dilute (>1%) NaOH solution, whirlimix, centrifuge, and decant, add 1-4 drops of safranin dye, top up with DI water, whirlimix, centrifuge, and decant
29. Transfer the pollen samples into small glass vials, centrifuge to remove excess water, and add 1-2 drops glycerol
30. Place a small drop of the pollen residue/glycerol mixture onto a slide, add a glass cover slip on top

Cryptotephra Extraction

Subsampling
1. Sample desired amount of sediment (ca. 1g) placed into a 15 ml plastic-bottomed centrifuge tube – record sample info in the Leeds Tephra Sample Log
2. Place in drying oven overnight with screw top on to minimize cross contaimination risk, but loose to allow water vapour release
Carbonate and fine sediment removal
3. Add 10 ml 10% HCl to each sample and leave for at least 1 hour
4. Sieve at 125 or 80 μm, using a new (uncontaminated) mesh washing through using tap water into a clean 1 l borosilicate beaker. For clay rich samples, a small amount of detergent can be added to aid deflocculation
5. Sieve the < 125 or 80 μm fraction, now in the beaker, at 25 or 15 μm
6. Rinse the 25/15-125/80 μm fraction from the mesh back into the centrifuge tube using tap water
7. Balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant
Silicate removal

8. Prepare suitable volume of SPT (sodium polytungstate) solution to a specific gravity ca. 1.95 and 2.5 g cm−3, allowing for ca. 8 ml per sample
9. Add 4 ml of the 1.95 g cm−3 SPT solution to each sample
10. Invert the test tubes until all sediment is suspended balance and centrifuge at 1800 rpm for 20 mins with the break off (deceleration >3)
11. Pour supernatant into a15 ml conical bottomed centrifuge tube, labelled “<1.95 g cm-3”
12. Repeat steps 9 to 11
13. Add 4 ml of the 2.5 g cm−3 SPT solution to each sample
14. Invert the test tubes until all sediment is suspended balance and centrifuge at 1800 rpm for 20 mins with the break off (deceleration >3)
15. Pour supernatant into a15 ml conical bottomed centrifuge tube, labelled “1.95 – 2.5 g cm-3”
16. Repeat steps 13 to 15
17. Top all tubes up with deionized water, centrifuge, and decant supernatant (SPT solution) into a labelled Schott bottle for recycling, repeat this process
Slide mounting
18. Turn on hot plate, and place Canada Balsam (opened) on the hot plate to warm
19. Re-sieve each 1.95 – 2.5 g cm-3 sample at 25 or 15 μm in the mini sieve to ensure removal of all SPT residues
20. Balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant
21. On the hotplate, place the sample, drop by drop, onto a frosted and clearly labelled microscope slide, allow all water to evaporate
22. Add a small amount (lentil-sized) of Canada Balsam to a microscope cover slip, and place this onto the sample

Heavy Mineral Extraction

Subsampling
1. Sample desired amount of sediment (ca. 1 cm3) using a volumetric sampler or using water displacement in a 5 cm3 measuring cylinder, placed into a 15 ml plastic centrifuge tube
Fine sediment removal
2. Sieve at 500 μm using DI water into a clean 1 l borosilicate beaker. For clay rich samples, a small amount of detergent can be added to aid deflocculation
3. Sieve the < 500 μm fraction, now in the beaker, at 63 μm
4. Rinse the 63-500 μm fraction from the sieve into the centrifuge tube
5. Balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant into a waste bottle
Acid digestion
6. Add 20 ml 25% HCl to each sample
7. Whirlimix, balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant
8. To remove the acid remainder whirlimix and top up with DI water
9. Balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant into a waste bottle
10. Add 20 ml 50% HNO3 to each sample, and repeat steps 4-6
Density separation
11. Prepare suitable volume of SPT (sodium polytungstate) solution to a specific gravity between 2.8 and 2.85 g cm−3, allowing for ca. 5 ml per sample
12. Add 4 ml of the prepared SPT solution to each sample
13. Whirlimix, balance and centrifuge at 1800 rpm for 20 mins with the break off (deceleration >3)
14. Pour off supernatant into a borosilicate beaker to prepare for SPT recycling
15. Top up centrifuge tube containing the remainder with DI water to within 5 ml of the top of the tube (reducing the overall density to around 1.15–1.2 g cm−3), cap, and mix by performing 8–10 inversions by hand 16. Repeat steps 9-12
Slide mounting 17. Turn on hot plate, and place Canada Balsam (opened) on the hot plate to warm
18. Add DI water to all samples to remove remaining SPT, balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant
19. On the hotplate, place the sample, drop by drop, onto a frosted and clearly labelled microscope slide, allow all water to evaporate
20. Add a small amount (lentil-sized) of Canada Balsam to a microscope cover slip, and place this onto the sample
