Subsampling 1. Sample desired amount of sediment (ca. 1 cm3) using a volumetric sampler or using water displacement in a 5 cm3 measuring cylinder, placed into a 15 ml plastic centrifuge tube 2. Add an exotic Lycopodium marker spore tablet to quantify the concentration of pollen within the samples Carbonate removal 3. Heat up water bath in fume hood to ca. 90°C 4. Add 30 ml 7% HCl to each sample and place in water bath for 20-30 mins, in clay rich samples stir occasionally with a glass rod (one per tube) 5. Whirlimix, balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant 6. To remove HCl remainder whirlimix and top up with DI water 7. Balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant into a waste bottle Humic acid and fine sediment removal 8. Add 25 ml 10% NaOH to each sample and place in water bath for 2-4 mins 9. Sieve at 125 μm, washing through using DI water into a clean 1 l borosilicate beaker. 10. Sieve the < 125 μm fraction, now in the beaker, at 6 μm. 11. Rinse the 6-125 μm fraction from the mesh back into the centrifuge tube using distilled water 12. Balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant Silicate removal 13. Prepare suitable volume of SPT (sodium polytungstate) solution to a specific gravity between 1.89 and 1.91 g cm−3, allowing for ca. 8 ml per sample 14. Add 4 ml of the prepared SPT solution to each sample 15. Whirlimix, balance and centrifuge at 1800 rpm for 20 mins with the break off (deceleration >3) 16. Pour supernatant into a new 50 ml nalgene centrifuge tube, topped up with DI water to within 5 ml of the top of the tube (reducing the overall density to around 1.15–1.2 g cm−3), cap, and mix by performing 8–10 inversions by hand 17. Repeat steps 14-16 18. Centrifuge tubes containing the supernatant at 3000 rpm for 3 mins. Decant supernatant into a 1 L borosilicate beaker for later recycling of SPT 19. Top up samples with deionized water and centrifuge and decant again Acetolysis 20. Whirlimix samples and top up with glacial acetic acid (CH3COOH) 21. Balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant into a waste bottle 22. Make up the acetolysis mixture by combining 9 parts of acetic anhydride with 1 part of conc. sulphuric acid 23. Place 5 ml of the acetolysis, working in the fume cupboard, and incubate in waterbath for 3 mins 24. Top up with glacial acetic acid (CH3COOH) to stop the reaction 25. Balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant into a waste bottle 26. To remove any acetolysis remainder whirlimix and top up with DI water 27. Balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant Slide mounting 28. OPTIONAL Wash with a dilute (>1%) NaOH solution, whirlimix, centrifuge, and decant, add 1-4 drops of safranin dye, top up with DI water, whirlimix, centrifuge, and decant 29. Transfer the pollen samples into small glass vials, centrifuge to remove excess water, and add 1-2 drops glycerol 30. Place a small drop of the pollen residue/glycerol mixture onto a slide, add a glass cover slip on top

Subsampling 1. Sample desired amount of sediment (ca. 1g) placed into a 15 ml plastic-bottomed centrifuge tube – record sample info in the Leeds Tephra Sample Log 2. Place in drying oven overnight with screw top on to minimize cross contaimination risk, but loose to allow water vapour release Carbonate and fine sediment removal 3. Add 10 ml 10% HCl to each sample and leave for at least 1 hour 4. Sieve at 125 or 80 μm, using a new (uncontaminated) mesh washing through using tap water into a clean 1 l borosilicate beaker. For clay rich samples, a small amount of detergent can be added to aid deflocculation 5. Sieve the < 125 or 80 μm fraction, now in the beaker, at 25 or 15 μm 6. Rinse the 25/15-125/80 μm fraction from the mesh back into the centrifuge tube using tap water 7. Balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant Silicate removal 8. Prepare suitable volume of SPT (sodium polytungstate) solution to a specific gravity ca. 1.95 and 2.5 g cm−3, allowing for ca. 8 ml per sample 9. Add 4 ml of the 1.95 g cm−3 SPT solution to each sample 10. Invert the test tubes until all sediment is suspended balance and centrifuge at 1800 rpm for 20 mins with the break off (deceleration >3) 11. Pour supernatant into a15 ml conical bottomed centrifuge tube, labelled “<1.95 g cm-3” 12. Repeat steps 9 to 11 13. Add 4 ml of the 2.5 g cm−3 SPT solution to each sample 14. Invert the test tubes until all sediment is suspended balance and centrifuge at 1800 rpm for 20 mins with the break off (deceleration >3) 15. Pour supernatant into a15 ml conical bottomed centrifuge tube, labelled “1.95 – 2.5 g cm-3” 16. Repeat steps 13 to 15 17. Top all tubes up with deionized water, centrifuge, and decant supernatant (SPT solution) into a labelled Schott bottle for recycling, repeat this process Slide mounting 18. Turn on hot plate, and place Canada Balsam (opened) on the hot plate to warm 19. Re-sieve each 1.95 – 2.5 g cm-3 sample at 25 or 15 μm in the mini sieve to ensure removal of all SPT residues 20. Balance and centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins, decant the supernatant 21. On the hotplate, place the sample, drop by drop, onto a frosted and clearly labelled microscope slide, allow all water to evaporate 22. Add a small amount (lentil-sized) of Canada Balsam to a microscope cover slip, and place this onto the sample
